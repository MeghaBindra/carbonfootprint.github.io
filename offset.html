What is a Carbon Offset?

A Carbon offset is a way to compensate for your emissions by funding an equivalent carbon dioxide saving elsewhere. Our everyday actions, at home and at work, consume energy and produce carbon emissions, such as driving, flying and heating buildings. Carbon offsetting is used to balance out these emissions by helping to pay for emission savings in other parts of the world.

Many of the carbon offsetting projects also provide wider benefits in addition to carbon reduction, such as biodiversity, education, jobs, food security and heath & well-being in developing countries.

General Usages

Turn off lights and appliances: Turn off the heavy appliances like heater, cooler, air conditioner, water heater to reduce more energy. Putting them to sleep is second best.

Turn down the heat: Use a programmable or smart thermostat, if you have one. Keep blinds closed to help keep temperature stable inside.

Replace your old light bulbs: LED lights use up to 85 percent less energy, last up to 25 times longer and are cheaper to run and saves more energy than usual light bulbs.

Choose renewable: If you live in a state where you can choose your energy supplier, pick one that runs on renewable.

Buy a laptop, not a desktop computer: Laptops take less energy to charge and run.

Install solar panels: Going solar is a great way to reduce your carbon footprint. By installing solar panels, you have the potential to offset up to 100% of your electricity usage.

Use waste RO filter water: You can reuse your waste filter water in your household cleaning or kitchen cleaning.

Travel

Drive Less: Try taking public transport such as train, bus or better ride a bike and for small distance choose to walk.

Go easy on fuel and brakes: Driving efficiently can help to reduce emissions. For instance, turn off your engine on traffic lights, always follow speed limits.

Service your car time to time to keep it more efficient.

Go with hybrid and electric cars: It is efficient to save fuel also environmental friendly.

Carpooling at least once a week.

Food Usage

Eat Less Meat: Cutting down on meat, and red meat in particular, is a better choice for the environment. For that reason, eating a vegan diet is likely to be best for the environment.

Choose organic: If you prefer to eat produce grown with fewer chemical pesticides, but when it comes to reducing your carbon footprint, you’re better off shifting to low-impact, plant-based foods.

What to Eat On: Skip the disposable dishes and wash your dinnerware instead. Washing dishes, whether it is by hand or in a dishwasher, is likely to be more environmentally friendly than using disposable ones.

Reduce plastic consumption: Take reusable coffee cups and water bottles with you each day, avoid produce that is wrapped in plastic, swap out kitchen and bathroom products to ones that come in biodegradable packaging.

Compost food scraps: Instead of throwing away food into general waste, consider composting it! This is a great source of nutrients for your garden, or pot plants.

Products

Buy less stuff: And buy used or recycled items whenever possible

Consider the fabric: Different materials have different environmental impacts, so that’s something to take into consideration too. Think wool over synthetics.

Donate old clothes: Better yet, use those too old to be donated for other purposes, like sewing projects or cleaning rags. Some animal shelters will take old sheets and towels for bedding material as well.

Wash your clothing in cold water: Doing two loads of laundry weekly in cold water instead of hot or warm water can save up to 500 pounds of carbon dioxide each year.

Take a reusable bag to the store.

Skip the packaging.

Invest in quality products that last.

Try more holistic remedies: Instead of taking prescription medications and antibiotics, try more holistic remedies.

Buy natural products: Use natural cleansers and moisturizers such as handmade soaps and coconut oil. Chemical manufacturing causes tons of carbon emissions so do your part to cut down.

Simple actions really add up when everyone joins in! By taking these small things into account you can reduce carbon emission easily and a step towards sustainable development
